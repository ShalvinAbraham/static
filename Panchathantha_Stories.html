The Loss of Friends

Once upon a time, in a forest, there was a wise lion named Simha. Simha had a lot of friends: birds, animals, and many other creatures in the forest. He was known to be powerful, strong, and a good leader. Everyone respected him. One day, while Simha was resting, a little mouse named Minoo came running to him in fear.

"What is it, Minoo?" the lion asked kindly.

"Oh, mighty Simha, I was running away from a cat that chased me", said the mouse, trembling.

The lion laughed. "Why are you afraid of such a small thing, little one?"

"I am small, but cats are strong!" replied Minoo.

Simha, with a smile, gently pushed the mouse to safety. "You are safe here. But remember, even small creatures have their value in this world".

Days passed, and the mouse and the lion became good friends. The mouse, who once feared the lion, would visit him often and help him with little things. The other animals in the forest noticed their unusual friendship.

One day, the lion got caught in a hunter's net. He struggled, but the more he tried, the tighter the ropes became. The lion roared for help, but none of the other animals dared to come close.

Just then, Minoo the mouse heard the lion’s roar and rushed to the scene. "Simha! Don't worry!" said the mouse. Using his sharp teeth, Minoo began to chew the ropes of the net. Slowly but surely, the mouse gnawed through the ropes, and Simha was free.

"Thank you, Minoo", said Simha gratefully. "You saved my life. I never imagined that you, such a tiny creature, would help me in such a big way".

The mouse smiled and replied, "Even the smallest friend can be of great help when you are in need. It's not size but friendship that matters".

From that day on, the lion and the mouse remained close friends. The other animals learned a valuable lesson from them – that true friends stand by each other, no matter how big or small.

No act of kindness, no matter how small, is ever wasted. True friendship knows no size.

The Wedge-Pulling Monkey

Once upon a time, there was a clever monkey named Chintu who lived in a large forest. Chintu was always full of energy and loved playing with his friends. One day, while hopping around the trees, he noticed something strange - a large wedge that seemed to have fallen from a tree stump.

Curious, Chintu approached the wedge and tried to move it, but it was stuck tightly in the ground. He tugged and pulled with all his strength, but the wedge wouldn't budge. Frustrated, Chintu decided to call his friend, Golu the elephant, for help.

Golu came charging through the forest, his large ears flapping as he arrived. "What seems to be the problem, Chintu?" he asked in a deep voice.

"This wedge is stuck in the ground", Chintu replied. "I tried pulling it, but it’s too strong for me".

Golu, being much stronger, tried to use his big trunk to pull the wedge out. But no matter how much he pushed and tugged, it still wouldn’t move. Golu and Chintu were both puzzled. What could they do?

Just then, a wise old owl named Babu flew down from a nearby tree. "Why don’t you both try working together?" Babu suggested. "Sometimes, the best solutions come when we combine our strengths". The animals thought about Babu’s suggestion and decided to give it a try.

Chintu and Golu stood on opposite sides of the wedge. Chintu grabbed the wedge with his little hands, while Golu used his trunk. Together, they pulled with all their might. And finally, with a loud *crack*, the wedge broke free from the ground!

Both Chintu and Golu laughed with joy, and thanked Babu the owl for his wise advice. They realized that when they worked together, they could accomplish much more than they could alone.

Teamwork makes the dream work! By helping each other, we can achieve great things.

The Jackal and the War-Drum

Once upon a time, in a dense forest, there lived a jackal named Kalu. Kalu was known to be clever but often hungry, and he was always on the lookout for an easy meal. One day, while wandering through the forest, he heard strange sounds coming from the distance. It sounded like loud drumming, and Kalu’s curiosity got the better of him.

Following the sound, he came upon a village where a group of soldiers was practicing for an upcoming battle. They were playing war-drums to prepare for the war. The drumming was so loud and intense that it shook the ground, and Kalu was both startled and intrigued. He watched from a distance, wondering what it was all about.

When the soldiers took a break, Kalu crept closer and found a large drum lying unattended. He had never seen such a big drum before, and his eyes sparkled with mischief. "What if I make some noise with this drum?" he thought. "It might scare away the animals, and I can steal some food in the chaos!"

Without wasting any time, Kalu jumped onto the drum and started banging it with his paws. The sound was thunderous, echoing through the forest. All the animals nearby were terrified. The birds flew away, the deer ran into the trees, and even the elephants trembled at the noise. Kalu laughed to himself, thinking he had caused quite a commotion.

But to his surprise, the soldiers came running back to the drum. They thought they were under attack and began to gather their weapons. The sound of the war-drum had tricked them into thinking the battle had begun. The soldiers quickly marched toward the village, preparing for war.

Realizing the trouble he had caused, Kalu quickly scurried away into the forest. He was scared and ashamed, as he had never expected the drum to cause such a mess. He learned that his cleverness had backfired, and now, all the animals in the forest were even more afraid of him than before.

From that day on, Kalu decided to use his cleverness for good and not cause trouble for others. He realized that sometimes, what seems like a clever idea can lead to unexpected consequences, and it’s important to think before acting.

Think before you act! Cleverness can be useful, but using it recklessly can cause more harm than good.

Merchant Strong-Tooth

Once upon a time, in a small town by the edge of a great forest, there lived a merchant named Strong-Tooth. He was called this name because he had the most impressive, shiny teeth in the town. Strong-Tooth was a kind man, known for being clever and fair. He sold all kinds of goods: spices, fabrics, and even rare stones from the far mountains.

One day, while traveling to a nearby village to trade, Strong-Tooth was stopped by a fox on the road. The fox, looking a bit tired and hungry, asked, "Merchant Strong-Tooth, would you spare a little food for me?"

The merchant, seeing the fox in such a state, smiled and said, "Of course, little one. I always help those in need". He gave the fox some bread and cheese and continued on his way.

The fox, feeling grateful, thanked Strong-Tooth and said, "You are a kind merchant. I would like to repay you for your generosity. There is a treasure hidden deep in the forest, guarded by a great lion. If you ever need riches, go to that place, and you will find gold and jewels beyond your wildest dreams".

Strong-Tooth, ever curious, decided to follow the fox’s advice. He set out into the forest, looking for the treasure. After a long journey, he came upon a large cave. The entrance was dark, but he could see the glint of gold inside. However, as he stepped closer, he heard a low growl. It was the lion, guarding the treasure.

The lion spoke in a deep voice, "Who dares to enter my domain?"

Without hesitation, Strong-Tooth said, "It is I, Merchant Strong-Tooth. I seek the treasure you guard".

The lion looked at him with keen eyes and said, "Many have come before you, but none have taken the treasure. You must prove that you are worthy to own it".

"And how do I prove my worth?" asked the merchant.

The lion grinned. "You must solve my riddle. If you answer correctly, the treasure is yours. But if you fail, you will never leave this cave".

The lion posed the riddle: "What has a heart that doesn’t beat, a mouth that doesn’t speak, and a body that never moves?"

Strong-Tooth thought carefully. He knew this was no ordinary riddle. After a few moments, a smile spread across his face. He replied, "A tree. A tree has a heart, its rings, but no heartbeat. It has a mouth, its branches, but it does not speak. And it has a body, its trunk, that does not move".

The lion was silent for a moment, then nodded, "You are indeed worthy. Take the treasure as you wish".

Strong-Tooth thanked the lion and filled his bag with gold and jewels. But as he left the cave, he remembered the fox’s advice, "Generosity brings riches". He realized that the true treasure wasn’t the gold, but the kindness and wisdom that had guided him along the way.

Generosity and wisdom are the greatest treasures. It’s not always the gold we find that matters, but the kindness we give.

Godly and June

Once upon a time, there were two friends named Godly and June. They lived in a small village nestled between tall mountains. Godly was a strong and kind man, while June was known for her cleverness and quick thinking. The two friends were always there for each other, and together, they were inseparable.

One day, as the sun rose high in the sky, the village elder called upon Godly and June for help. "There is a great storm coming, and we need to prepare the village for it", the elder said, his voice full of concern.

Without hesitation, Godly and June agreed to help. They gathered the villagers and began working together to secure their homes and gather enough food and supplies for the storm.

As the storm clouds gathered and the winds started to howl, the villagers worked hard, securing their homes with wooden planks and tying down loose objects. But despite all the preparations, the storm was far more powerful than anyone had anticipated. The rain poured down in sheets, and the wind howled with fury, knocking over trees and flooding the fields.

In the midst of the storm, Godly and June found themselves separated. The wind blew so hard that it was impossible to see or hear anything clearly. June, with her quick thinking, took shelter inside a cave, while Godly fought to help the villagers in the open. Hours passed, and the storm grew stronger.

Finally, as the storm began to subside, June ventured out from the cave and saw Godly standing in the village square, drenched and tired but still smiling. "Godly!" June called, running toward him. "Are you alright?"

Godly smiled and said, "I’m fine, thanks to your quick thinking, June. You were right to seek shelter. But I couldn’t stop thinking about the villagers. Many of them need help, and we must make sure they’re safe".

Together, they went from house to house, checking on the villagers and making sure everyone was safe. Despite the damage to their homes and crops, the villagers were all alive and well, thanks to the hard work and dedication of Godly and June.

When the storm finally passed and the skies cleared, the villagers gathered to thank their two heroes. "We could not have made it through without your courage and quick thinking", the elder said. "You both showed us that no matter how fierce the storm, we can overcome it together".

Godly and June looked at each other and smiled. They knew that true strength was not in how strong you were or how quick you could think, but in the willingness to help others and work together in times of need.

True strength lies not in individual power, but in working together and helping others in times of need.

The Jackal at the Ram-Fight

In a dense forest, there lived a jackal named Jimo. Jimo was known for his sly nature and his ability to escape tricky situations. He was clever, but sometimes too much for his own good.

One day, while wandering through the forest, Jimo overheard a group of animals talking excitedly. "The great ram-fight is happening today!" they said. "It’s going to be the fiercest battle of the season!"

Curious, Jimo followed the sounds of the chatter until he reached a clearing where a huge crowd of animals had gathered. In the middle of the clearing, two mighty rams were preparing for a fight. The rams had been rivals for years, and this fight would settle who was the strongest once and for all.

Jimo saw an opportunity. "What if I could win the fight without even participating?" he thought to himself. He quickly came up with a plan. He dashed into the crowd and pretended to be one of the spectators, watching the fight with great enthusiasm.

The battle began, and the rams charged at each other, locking horns and pushing with all their might. The animals around the clearing cheered, shouting for their favorite ram. But Jimo had something else in mind. As the rams struggled, Jimo slipped behind one of them and bit its tail.

Startled by the sudden pain, the ram let out a loud bleat and buckled, losing its footing. The other ram, seeing its chance, charged forward and knocked its opponent to the ground.

The crowd went wild, cheering for the victorious ram. But Jimo wasn’t done yet. As the defeated ram limped away, Jimo dashed up to it and whispered, "You may have lost today, but I can help you get revenge". The ram, confused but intrigued, nodded slowly.

Jimo told the ram that he had a secret plan to make the winner look bad. "We’ll trick everyone into thinking the battle is still ongoing. You just have to wait for my signal", Jimo said with a sly grin.

The defeated ram agreed, and together, they put Jimo’s plan into action. The next day, they returned to the clearing and pretended that the fight was still in progress. The crowd gathered once again, eager to see the outcome of the rematch.

When the rams faced off again, Jimo jumped into the middle of the fight, causing a commotion. He tricked the other animals into thinking that the fight had gotten out of control. The confusion was enough to make the victorious ram appear weak and unsure.

The animals, confused and uncertain, began to question the winner’s strength. Jimo’s plan had worked, and the defeated ram was seen as the true champion.

But in the end, the trickery didn’t last long. The animals realized what had happened and soon saw through Jimo’s deception. They turned on him and kicked him out of the clearing, leaving him to face the consequences of his actions.

Jimo, ashamed of his dishonesty, learned a valuable lesson: trickery may win in the short term, but honesty and fairness always triumph in the end.

Trickery may give you a short-term advantage, but honesty and fairness will always lead to greater respect and success.

The Weaver's Wife

In a peaceful village by the riverbank, there lived a humble weaver named Vasant. He was known for his fine cloth, which he wove from silk and cotton. Vasant worked tirelessly each day, creating beautiful fabrics that were sold to the villagers and travelers. His wife, Leela, was a loving and kind woman, always supporting him with her gentle words and sweet smile.

One day, as Vasant was weaving a particularly intricate design, he noticed that his cloth was not as smooth as usual. It had uneven patches and knots that made it difficult to weave. He tried again and again, but each attempt was met with failure. Frustrated, he took a break and sat down to think.

"What is wrong with me?" he muttered. "I have always been able to weave the finest cloth, but now I cannot even finish a simple piece".

Leela, who had been listening, came over and gently placed a hand on his shoulder. "Vasant, do not be so hard on yourself. Sometimes, we all face challenges, and it is in these moments that we grow stronger. Take a deep breath, clear your mind, and try again".

Vasant smiled at his wife's kind words. He decided to take her advice and rest for a while. He walked outside to the garden, where the flowers bloomed in full color, and the gentle breeze swayed the trees. He sat quietly, feeling the peace of nature surrounding him.

As he sat, a thought came to him. "I have been focusing too much on the mistakes. I need to remember what brought me joy in weaving – the beauty of creating something with my hands". With this new perspective, Vasant returned to his loom, but this time, he focused on the process, not just the result.

With each pass of the thread, his mind grew calm, and the weaving became easier. The cloth began to take shape, smooth and beautiful once again. He felt proud of his work, not because it was perfect, but because he had persevered and learned from his challenges.

Leela came to check on him and smiled when she saw the cloth. "I knew you could do it", she said. "You just needed to find the right balance". Vasant nodded, grateful for her unwavering belief in him.

From that day on, Vasant realized that life was much like weaving – sometimes, we face obstacles that seem impossible to overcome. But with patience, persistence, and the support of those we love, we can always find our way back to success.

True strength lies not in perfection, but in the ability to persist and learn from our challenges.

How the Crow-Hen Killed the Black Snake

Once upon a time, in a lush green forest, a black snake named Kalin was feared by all the creatures. Kalin was known for her strength and speed, and no one dared to cross her path. She lived near the river, where she would slither and strike fear into the hearts of smaller animals.

Among the many animals living nearby was a clever crow-hen named Meera. She was a small, unassuming bird, but she had a sharp mind and was known for her wisdom. Meera had a family of chicks, and she was determined to keep them safe from the dangers lurking in the forest.

One afternoon, as Meera was collecting food for her chicks, she noticed the black snake, Kalin, slithering dangerously close to her nest. The sight of the snake made her heart race. Kalin was infamous for eating small animals and birds, and Meera feared for the safety of her young ones.

Meera quickly flew up to a tree branch and watched as the snake slithered past. Kalin seemed to be unaware of Meera’s presence, but the crow-hen knew she had to act quickly. She had an idea and decided to put her cleverness to use.

Meera began to caw loudly, drawing Kalin's attention. The snake, hearing the noise, raised its head and looked up at the crow-hen perched high in the tree.

"Why are you disturbing me, little bird?" hissed Kalin, her eyes narrowing in anger.

"I was just thinking about how foolish you are, snake", Meera replied boldly. "Do you not know that there are creatures in this forest far more powerful than you? Surely, you wouldn’t want to face them".

The snake was curious. "What are you talking about?" she asked.

Meera smirked and said, "There is a mighty beast that roams the forest at night, even stronger and fiercer than you. It has sharp claws and teeth that can tear through anything. It is said that this beast once defeated a whole army of snakes!"

Now intrigued, Kalin’s interest was piqued. "Where is this beast?" she asked eagerly, trying to gather more information.

Meera continued, "I can lead you to it, but you must promise not to harm me in return". The crow-hen knew that the snake’s pride would make her want to challenge this so-called "beast".

The snake agreed, her curiosity getting the best of her. "Lead the way, little bird", she said.

Meera flew down and started leading Kalin through the forest. She made sure to keep just out of reach of the snake, always keeping a distance. After a while, they reached a dense part of the forest where the ground was covered in thick vines and tall grass.

Meera stopped and pointed to the shadows. "There! Do you see it?"

Kalin squinted, but all she could see was the dark outline of a large bush. "That is just a bush!" she hissed. "You have tricked me!"

But just then, the snake heard a rustling in the bushes. Startled, she turned around to find a group of other snakes emerging from the shadows. They were the true masters of the forest, much larger and more dangerous than Kalin herself!

Kalin’s pride was shattered. She realized she had been lured into a trap, and in her arrogance, she had walked straight into the den of her enemies.

The snakes surrounded Kalin, and with their combined strength, they defeated her. Meera, having outsmarted the black snake, returned to her nest with her chicks, safe once again.

From that day on, the animals of the forest knew better than to underestimate the power of a small, clever creature. Meera had used her intelligence to save her family and the entire forest from the danger of Kalin.

Wisdom and cleverness can overcome even the greatest of challenges. Sometimes, it’s not strength, but intelligence, that wins the battle.

The Heron that Liked Crab-Meat

Once upon a time, in a peaceful lake surrounded by tall grass, lived a heron named Hari. Hari was a graceful bird, known for his long legs and sharp beak, perfect for catching fish and crabs. However, Hari had a secret – he loved eating crab-meat more than anything else in the world. It was his favorite treat, and he would search high and low for it.

One day, as he was wandering along the shore, he spotted a large, plump crab hiding near a rock. Hari's mouth watered at the thought of the delicious crab-meat. He bent down and caught the crab in his beak, but instead of eating it right away, he thought of something clever.

"Why should I eat this crab all by myself?" Hari wondered. "Perhaps I can share it with my friends and make them think I am kind and generous".

Hari took the crab to his favorite spot by the lake, where he often rested. He began to cry out, "Oh, how sad I am! There is no one in this world who appreciates the taste of crab-meat. It is such a rare delicacy, and I have so much of it, yet I have no one to share it with!"

Hearing Hari's cries, a wise old turtle named Tanu approached him. Tanu had lived in the lake for many years and had seen many things. "Why are you so sad, Hari?" the turtle asked gently.

"Oh, Tanu", Hari replied, "I have found the most delicious crab-meat, but I am too old to enjoy it. My beak is no longer sharp enough, and my legs are too tired to hunt for more. I wish I had someone to share it with, but alas, I am alone".

Tanu, feeling pity for the heron, said, "You are a kind bird, Hari, and I would be honored to share the crab-meat with you. After all, we both deserve such a treat". Tanu slowly waddled over to where Hari had placed the crab and prepared to enjoy it.

Just as the turtle was about to take a bite, Hari quickly swooped down and grabbed the crab with his beak, pulling it away from the turtle. "Ah, Tanu, I’m afraid this crab is too precious to share. I simply cannot part with it", Hari said, pretending to feel guilty.

Tanu was taken aback by the heron’s sudden change of heart. "But you said you were too old to enjoy it", Tanu replied, feeling disappointed. "Why would you lie to me?"

Hari, now feeling a little guilty, explained, "I confess, I love crab-meat more than anything else. I wanted to trick you into thinking I was being generous, so I could enjoy it all by myself. But I have learned that it is not right to deceive others for my own benefit".

Tanu nodded wisely and said, "It is true, Hari, that everyone loves good food. But a wise person shares what they have with others, rather than keeping it all for themselves. A kind heart is worth more than a full stomach".

Hari realized the truth in Tanu’s words. From that day on, he stopped deceiving others and shared his meals with his friends. He learned that generosity and kindness were far more rewarding than selfishness.

True happiness comes from sharing with others. A generous heart is more valuable than all the crab-meat in the world.

Numskull and the Rabbit

Once upon a time, in a vast jungle, there lived a powerful lion named Numskull. He was the king of the jungle and ruled over all the animals. However, Numskull was very proud of his strength and often boasted about it. He believed that no animal could match his might and that he could easily defeat any creature in his kingdom.

One day, while roaming the jungle, Numskull encountered a small rabbit named Moti. The rabbit, who was known for his quick thinking, had heard of the lion's boasting and was not afraid. The lion roared, "I am the mightiest of all, and no one dares challenge me! I can crush any creature with a single swipe of my paw!"

Moti, who was quick-witted, said, "Oh mighty Numskull, I have heard many tales of your strength, but I wonder, can you defeat me?"

Numskull laughed loudly, "You? A small rabbit? How can you even think of challenging me? I could crush you without even trying!"

"Perhaps, but if you can defeat me, I have a great prize for you", Moti said, with a twinkle in his eye. "If you beat me, I will lead you to a hidden treasure, one that no other animal in the jungle knows about". Moti knew that Numskull's greed and pride would make him accept the challenge.

The lion, eager to prove his strength and gain the treasure, agreed to the challenge. Moti then led the lion to a deep, dark cave, which was actually the home of an old, wise tortoise. Inside the cave, there was a large, circular pit filled with thick mud.

"This is where we will have the challenge", Moti said. "You must jump across the pit to the other side. If you can do it, you will have your treasure". Numskull, eager to show off, agreed to the challenge without hesitation. He took a running start and leaped into the air.

However, as he landed on the other side, his paws slipped in the mud, and he fell into the pit. The thick mud trapped him, and no matter how hard he tried, he couldn't escape. The more he struggled, the deeper he sank.

Moti stood at the edge of the pit, watching with a sly smile. "It seems that even the mighty Numskull can be defeated by a small rabbit like me", he said, chuckling. "The treasure was never real, Numskull. I only wanted to show you that true strength does not lie in physical power alone, but in wisdom and wit". The rabbit had outsmarted the lion!

After a while, some of the other animals came to help, and together they pulled Numskull out of the pit. The lion, covered in mud and humbled by the rabbit's cleverness, learned an important lesson that day. He realized that strength alone was not enough, and that intelligence and humility were just as important.

True strength is not always about physical power. Wisdom and humility can often outsmart brute force.

The Weaver Who Loved a Princess

Once upon a time, in a bustling village, there lived a humble and talented weaver named Nanu. Nanu was known far and wide for his ability to weave the finest cloths. His creations were admired by all, but despite his skill, Nanu was lonely. He had one dream: to win the heart of the beautiful Princess Sita, who lived in the royal palace.

One day, Nanu heard that the princess was to hold a grand festival to celebrate the harvest. The village was abuzz with excitement, and Nanu saw this as the perfect opportunity to show the princess his talents. He decided to weave the most intricate and beautiful fabric the world had ever seen, one that would surely impress her.

For days and nights, Nanu worked tirelessly in his small workshop. He used the finest silks, the brightest dyes, and the most delicate patterns. Finally, after much effort, he completed a beautiful cloth that shimmered with colors like the sunset. Proud of his work, Nanu packed the cloth and made his way to the palace.

When Nanu arrived at the palace, he presented the cloth to the princess, who was sitting on her throne. She was taken aback by the beauty of the fabric. "This is the finest cloth I have ever seen", she said. "But what do you ask in return for such a beautiful gift?"

Nanu, with a nervous heart, replied, "Your highness, I do not ask for gold or jewels. I only ask for your hand in marriage, for I have loved you from afar for many years".

The princess was silent for a moment. She looked at Nanu, then at the beautiful cloth, and said, "Your work is remarkable, but love cannot be won by gifts alone. You must prove your worthiness in other ways".

Disheartened but not defeated, Nanu returned to his village. He decided to work harder and prove his worth by helping those in need. He spent his days aiding the sick, feeding the hungry, and building homes for the poor. As the months passed, the people of the village began to admire him not just for his weaving, but for his kindness and generosity.

News of Nanu’s good deeds spread throughout the kingdom, and soon the princess heard of his efforts. One day, she invited him to the palace once more. This time, Nanu did not bring fabric, but his heart full of sincerity. The princess, moved by his selflessness, said, "You have shown me that true love is not about fine things, but about the goodness in a person’s heart. I will marry you, Nanu".

And so, the humble weaver and the princess were married. They lived happily ever after, proving that true love is not won by material gifts, but by kindness, hard work, and sincerity.

True love is not won by material things, but by kindness, sincerity, and selfless deeds.

The Ungrateful Man

Once upon a time, in a small village, there was a kind-hearted merchant named Rajan. Rajan was known for his generosity and helpfulness. One day, as he was traveling through the forest, he came across a man who had fallen into a deep pit. The man was in great distress and unable to climb out on his own.

Rajan, without hesitation, approached the pit and offered his hand to help the man. With much effort, Rajan pulled the man out of the pit, saving him from a dangerous situation. The man was grateful and thanked Rajan profusely. However, Rajan, being a humble man, only smiled and said, "It was nothing. I am happy to help".

As they walked through the forest together, the man shared his story. He explained that he had been wandering for days, lost and without food. Rajan, feeling pity for the man, offered him food from his own bag and even gave him a small amount of money to help with his journey.

The man accepted the help but was secretly unhappy. He thought to himself, "This man is too kind. I should take more from him. Why should I suffer when he has so much?" So, he began to plot a way to take advantage of Rajan’s generosity.

When they reached the village, the man turned to Rajan and said, "Thank you for your help, but I cannot stay here any longer. I must continue my journey". Rajan wished him well and turned to leave. However, just as Rajan was about to depart, the man called out, "Wait! I need more help. You are a kind man, and I am in desperate need of more money. Could you help me again?"

Rajan, surprised by the request, looked at the man and said, "I have already helped you once. I cannot give you more". But the man, with a fake look of despair, insisted, "You must help me. After all, you are the one who saved me from the pit. Surely you will not abandon me now".

Rajan hesitated for a moment but then shook his head and said, "No, I have already done what I could. You must learn to help yourself now". With that, he turned away and walked toward his home.

As Rajan walked away, the ungrateful man stood there, furious. He shouted after Rajan, "You are a fool! I will remember this day!" But Rajan, without looking back, continued on his way, knowing that his kindness had been enough, and the man’s ingratitude was his burden to bear.

The man’s true nature was revealed, and he wandered the land, never finding true friendship or happiness. He learned too late that gratitude and humility are virtues that open the door to kindness and good fortune.

Gratitude is a virtue that turns kindness into a lasting bond. An ungrateful heart can never find true happiness.

Leap and Creep

Once upon a time, in a lush green forest, there lived two friends - a rabbit named Leap and a tortoise named Creep. Leap, as his name suggested, was quick and energetic, always hopping around from one place to another. Creep, on the other hand, was slow and steady. He took his time and never hurried, enjoying the peaceful pace of life.

One day, Leap and Creep were sitting under a tree when they overheard some animals talking about a race. "Who do you think will win the race?" asked the squirrel. "Certainly not Creep!" laughed the deer. "Leap is far too fast for him", agreed the rabbit. Leap overheard this conversation and proudly declared, "I will race anyone in this forest and win easily!"

Creep, who was sitting quietly, looked up and said, "I’ll race you, Leap". The animals in the forest were shocked. They couldn’t imagine the slow tortoise keeping up with the speedy rabbit. "Are you sure about this, Creep?" asked the squirrel. "You’re so slow, and Leap is much faster than you!"

One day, they heard a rumor about an ancient treasure hidden deep in the forest. Greedy for wealth, they decided to embark on a journey to find it.

The first friend was a scholar who had studied ancient texts. He claimed, "I can decipher any script and read the maps that lead to the treasure". The second friend was a skilled tracker who boasted, "I can navigate any terrain and find the safest path". The third friend was a magician who said, "With my powers, I can protect us from danger". The fourth friend, however, was a simple man with common sense. He did not possess any extraordinary skill, but he was wise.

The four set out on their journey, and after traveling for days, they reached a deep forest. As they ventured further, they encountered wild animals and treacherous paths, but with the tracker’s guidance and the magician’s spells, they overcame every obstacle.

One day, they stumbled upon an ancient inscription carved into a stone. The scholar eagerly examined it and translated the words. "The treasure is buried beneath the old banyan tree, but beware, for greed may lead to destruction", he read aloud.

Excited, they rushed to find the banyan tree. After digging for hours, they finally uncovered a large chest. Their eyes gleamed with excitement as they opened it, revealing gold coins, jewels, and priceless artifacts. "We are rich!" they exclaimed.

However, as they celebrated, a thought crept into their minds - who would take the largest share? The scholar argued, "Without my knowledge, we would have never found the treasure. I deserve the biggest portion". The tracker countered, "But without me, we wouldn't have reached this place. I should get the most". The magician added, "If I had not protected us with my magic, we would not have survived. The largest share should be mine!"

The three friends began quarreling, their voices rising in anger. The fourth friend, who had remained silent, watched them carefully. He realized that their greed was blinding them. "Brothers", he said, "we should divide the treasure equally and return home safely. Fighting among ourselves will only lead to ruin".

But the others ignored him. Blinded by greed, they turned against each other. The magician, using his powers, tried to cast a spell to weaken the others. The scholar, knowing ancient secrets, chanted counter-spells. The tracker, using his strength, attacked them both. In their rage, they failed to notice a group of bandits hiding in the shadows.

The bandits had been watching them all along. Seeing their opportunity, they rushed in, overpowered the exhausted friends, and took the treasure. The three greedy friends were left empty-handed, bruised, and ashamed.

The fourth friend, who had stayed away from the fight, approached them and said, "You see? Greed led to your downfall. Had you listened to reason, we would have all shared the wealth and returned safely. But now, because of your selfishness, we have lost everything".

Realizing their mistake, the three friends hung their heads in shame. They had let their greed destroy their friendship and their chance at fortune.

With heavy hearts, they returned home, wiser but poorer. They learned that knowledge, strength, and magic are useless if they are not accompanied by wisdom and humility.

Greed and selfishness can lead to destruction, while wisdom and fairness bring true success.

The Lion-Makers

Once upon a time, in a distant land, there lived four friends who had grown up together. Three of them were highly educated, having spent years mastering various sciences and scriptures. They prided themselves on their intelligence and considered themselves superior to others. The fourth friend, however, was not learned in books but was wise and had good common sense.

One day, the three scholars decided to travel to seek the patronage of a wealthy king who would reward them for their knowledge. They invited their uneducated friend to join them, but they often mocked him, saying, "What use is he? He has no learning, no knowledge. He cannot help us".

Despite their arrogance, the fourth friend remained humble and accompanied them on their journey. As they walked through the forest, they stumbled upon the remains of a dead lion.

The first scholar, excited at the opportunity to display his knowledge, said, "My studies have taught me how to reassemble bones. I will put this skeleton back together". He recited complex formulas, and within moments, the bones arranged themselves into the perfect form of a lion.

The second scholar then declared, "I have studied how to give flesh and blood to lifeless forms". He chanted powerful mantras, and the lion’s body became complete, covered in flesh, skin, and a beautiful mane.

The third scholar, eager to prove his superior intelligence, proclaimed, "I can give this creature life!" He began to prepare his final incantation.

At this moment, the fourth friend, who had been silently watching, suddenly stepped forward and cried, "Stop! Have you lost your minds? This is a lion! If you bring it to life, it will kill us all!"

The three scholars laughed at him. "Fool! You do not understand the power of knowledge. You are just a simpleton with no education. Stand aside and watch as we achieve the impossible!"

Realizing that his friends would not listen, the wise man quickly climbed a nearby tree and hid among the branches.

The third scholar completed his chant, and the lion’s eyes opened. It took a deep breath, roared mightily, and, seeing the three men before it, attacked them instantly. The scholars, despite all their knowledge, had no time to react. The lion pounced on them and tore them apart.

From his safe perch in the tree, the fourth friend watched in sorrow. His three companions, who had prided themselves on their knowledge, had perished because they lacked the wisdom to use their learning wisely.

Once the lion had left, the wise man climbed down, mourned his friends, and continued his journey alone. In the end, it was not education but common sense that had saved him.

Knowledge without wisdom can lead to destruction.

One day, the three friends decided to travel to a faraway kingdom to seek their fortune. "With our combined intelligence, we will surely become rich and powerful", boasted Thousand-Wit. "Even Single-Wit will benefit from our journey".

Excited by the adventure, they packed their belongings and set off through the dense forest. The journey was long and challenging, and as they walked, they encountered various difficulties - crossing rivers, climbing hills, and avoiding wild animals. Each time, Hundred-Wit and Thousand-Wit used their intelligence to find solutions, while Single-Wit simply followed along.

"This bird is no ordinary creature", said Hundred-Wit. "If we capture it, we can sell it to the king and earn a fortune".

Thousand-Wit agreed, saying, "I will use my great intelligence to trap the bird". He quickly devised a complicated plan involving nets and ropes. Hundred-Wit added his own clever ideas, and together, they set up a trap.

Single-Wit, however, felt uneasy. "Maybe we should leave the bird alone", he said hesitantly. "What if it belongs to someone powerful? Or what if it brings bad luck?"

The three friends were astonished. A talking bird! This made it even more valuable. "We will take it to the king at once", declared Thousand-Wit.

However, as they journeyed onward with the bird in a cage, they unknowingly entered the territory of a powerful demon. The demon, seeing the golden bird, let out a thunderous roar. "Who dares to take my sacred bird?" it bellowed.

The three friends trembled with fear. The demon was enormous, with eyes like burning coals and teeth as sharp as daggers. "We - we did not know it belonged to you", stammered Hundred-Wit.

"You will pay for your greed!" the demon thundered. With a single swipe of his clawed hand, he struck down Hundred-Wit and Thousand-Wit. Their intelligence had failed them, for they had not foreseen the consequences of their actions.

Single-Wit, trembling, fell to his knees. "Great lord, I am but a simple man. I did not wish to harm your bird. Please spare me!"

The demon studied him for a moment and then let out a deep laugh. "You are honest and humble. You may live". With that, the demon vanished into the shadows, taking the golden bird with him.

Single-Wit, shaken but alive, looked at his fallen friends. Their brilliance had led them to ruin, while his simple heart had saved him. He realized that intelligence alone was not enough - wisdom and humility were just as important.

With a heavy heart, he buried his friends and continued his journey alone, carrying with him the greatest lesson of all: true wisdom lies not just in wit, but in understanding the consequences of one’s actions.

Intelligence without wisdom can lead to destruction, while humility and caution can save a person from danger.

The Musical Donkey

Once upon a time, in a small village, there lived a washerman who owned a donkey. The donkey worked hard, carrying heavy loads of clothes from the river to the washerman’s house every day. Though the donkey was loyal and diligent, he was often underfed and grew thin and weak.

One day, as the donkey wandered near the outskirts of the village in search of food, he met a cunning jackal. The jackal, unlike the donkey, was sly and always looking for ways to get food without working hard.

"Friend, why do you look so tired and hungry?" the jackal asked.

"My master barely feeds me", sighed the donkey. "I work all day, yet my stomach remains empty".

The jackal grinned. "Why don’t you join me? I know of a lush farm nearby filled with delicious vegetables and juicy crops. If we go at night, no one will catch us, and we can feast until we are full".

The donkey hesitated, knowing it was wrong to steal. But his hunger was stronger than his morals, and he agreed.

That night, the donkey followed the jackal to a nearby farm. The sight was breathtaking - fresh cucumbers, ripe pumpkins, and leafy greens covered the field. The donkey and the jackal ate to their hearts’ content. This became their nightly routine, and soon, the donkey grew healthy and fat.

One night, after a particularly satisfying meal, the donkey felt so happy that he couldn’t contain himself. "Friend, I feel like singing!" he said joyfully.

The jackal’s eyes widened in alarm. "Are you mad? If you sing, the farmer will wake up and catch us!"

"Nonsense!" said the donkey, puffing up his chest. "A fine voice should not be wasted. It is a gift to the world".

The jackal, realizing that the stubborn donkey would not listen, quickly came up with a plan. "Very well", he said. "But before you sing, let me step outside the farm so I can keep watch. I’ll warn you if someone comes".

The donkey, pleased that his friend respected his talent, nodded. As soon as the jackal slipped out of the farm, the donkey threw back his head and began to bray loudly, completely absorbed in his ‘musical performance.’

The loud, harsh braying shattered the peaceful night, waking the farmer and his helpers. "Thieves!" the farmer shouted, grabbing a thick stick. His men rushed into the farm and saw the donkey standing there, still lost in his song.

Before the donkey could escape, the furious farmer and his helpers caught him and gave him a sound beating. Bruised and battered, the donkey barely managed to limp away as the jackal watched from a safe distance, shaking his head in amusement.

When the donkey finally reached him, the jackal smirked and said, "Friend, I warned you, but you refused to listen. This is what happens when you ignore good advice".

The donkey, now sore and regretful, had no choice but to admit his mistake. From that day on, he never sang while stealing food again.

There is a right time and place for everything. Acting foolishly without thinking of the consequences can lead to trouble.

Slow, the Weaver

Once upon a time, in a bustling village, there lived a weaver named Slow. He was known throughout the village not for his skill in weaving, but for his laziness and lack of ambition. While other weavers worked hard to earn their livelihood, Slow spent his time daydreaming about wealth and luxury.

One day, while walking near the river, he saw a strange sight. A beautiful, golden-colored tortoise was resting on the riverbank. Slow had never seen such a creature before. Curious, he approached the tortoise, who spoke in a gentle voice, "Good day, traveler. Why do you look so troubled?"

Slow, surprised that the tortoise could talk, told him about his struggles. "I wish to be rich without working hard. I see others toil all day, but I don’t have the patience for it. Is there an easier way to become wealthy?"

The tortoise smiled wisely. "If you truly desire wealth, I can give you a magical thread. This thread will weave itself into the finest cloth, and you can sell it for great riches. But remember, you must be patient and not be greedy".

Slow eagerly agreed. The tortoise handed him a glowing golden thread and disappeared into the river. Excited, Slow rushed back to his workshop and set up his loom. As soon as he placed the magical thread on the loom, it started weaving on its own, creating the most exquisite fabric anyone had ever seen.

He took the cloth to the marketplace, where merchants gasped at its beauty. A wealthy trader bought it for a great sum, making Slow richer than he had ever been. Overjoyed, he continued using the magical thread, and soon, he had a grand house, fine clothes, and plenty of gold.

However, greed began to take over his heart. "If I can weave this much fabric with one thread, imagine how much I can create if I have more!" he thought. Ignoring the tortoise’s warning, he returned to the riverbank and called out, "Oh wise tortoise! I need more magical thread!"

The tortoise appeared and frowned. "You have already gained much wealth. Why do you desire more?"

"I want to be the richest man in the kingdom!" Slow declared.

The tortoise sighed but handed him another thread, warning, "Greed can be dangerous. Use this wisely".

Slow hurried back and placed both threads on his loom. The loom began weaving at an incredible speed, faster than he could manage. Soon, the entire room filled with cloth, leaving no space for him to move. Panicking, Slow tried to stop the loom, but it would not listen. The cloth kept growing, piling up until it burst out of the house, destroying everything in its path.

Realizing his mistake, Slow ran back to the river and begged the tortoise for help. The tortoise shook his head. "I warned you about greed, but you did not listen. Now, you must learn your lesson".

With that, the tortoise vanished, and Slow was left with nothing but ruins. Having lost everything due to his greed, he finally understood the value of patience and hard work. From that day on, he worked diligently as a weaver, earning an honest living.

Greed can lead to destruction. Patience and hard work bring lasting rewards.

A voice from the tree spoke, "If you take this golden fruit, all your desires will be fulfilled. You will have wealth, fame, and everything your heart desires".

Greed can lead to destruction. True wealth is found in wisdom, contentment, and humility.

The Unforgiving Monkey

In a lush forest, there lived a monkey named Miku. He was known for his cleverness and wit. He loved to play pranks on the other animals in the forest, and his mischievous ways often caused trouble. But no one ever dared to scold Miku, for he was quick to escape and would always find a way to laugh off the consequences of his actions.

One day, as Miku swung from tree to tree, he saw a crow perched on a branch. The crow was calmly preening its feathers, unaware of the monkey’s presence. Miku’s eyes sparkled with mischief, and he decided to play a prank on the unsuspecting crow.

He picked up a large, juicy fruit from the ground and, without thinking, threw it straight at the crow. The fruit hit the crow on the back, causing it to squawk loudly and flap its wings in surprise. The crow turned around, looking angrily at the monkey, who was grinning from ear to ear.

"Why did you do that?" the crow asked, clearly irritated. "What harm have I done to you?"

"Oh, I was just having a bit of fun!" Miku chuckled. "You look so serious all the time, so I thought I’d give you a little excitement".

The crow flapped its wings in annoyance. "You are always causing trouble. I don’t find your pranks amusing, Miku. I am going to teach you a lesson".

"Oh, go ahead", Miku said nonchalantly. "I’m not afraid of you".

The crow, however, was determined. It flew away, and soon Miku heard a rustling sound in the bushes. The next moment, a giant snake slithered out of the bushes and coiled around the base of the tree Miku was perched on.

"What is this?" Miku thought, startled. "I didn’t know there was a snake here!"

The crow returned, perched on a nearby branch, and spoke, "Miku, you have been causing trouble for everyone in the forest. Now, you must face the consequences of your actions. The snake will take care of you".

Miku’s heart raced. He had underestimated the crow and was now trapped. The snake hissed and began to move closer to the monkey’s tree. Miku, realizing the gravity of the situation, started to panic.

But then, an idea struck him. He called out to the crow, "Wait! I am sorry for what I did. I didn’t mean to hurt you. Please forgive me, and I will make it up to you!"

The crow hesitated for a moment, but then it said, "You have caused me harm, Miku. How can I trust that you are truly sorry?"

"I swear I am sorry!" Miku pleaded. "I won’t prank anyone again. Please, don’t let the snake hurt me".

The crow thought for a moment and then flew down to where Miku was perched. "Very well", it said. "I will forgive you this time. But remember, forgiveness should not be given lightly. You must show that you have changed".

The crow then called the snake off, and the snake slithered away, much to Miku’s relief. Miku, grateful for the crow’s mercy, promised to change his ways.

From that day on, Miku no longer played pranks on the other animals. He learned that while a good joke can bring laughter, causing harm to others can lead to consequences. The crow had taught him the importance of forgiveness and how one must earn it through sincerity and change.

Forgiveness is a powerful gift, but it should not be taken for granted. True change must follow sincere apologies.

The Fiend Who Washed His Feet

One evening, as Raghav was walking through the forest, he came across an old man sitting by the roadside. The old man was poor and wore tattered clothes, but his face radiated peace and wisdom. Seeing the old man, Raghav decided to play a cruel trick on him.

He walked up to the old man and said, "You seem tired and dusty from your journey. Would you like me to wash your feet and make you feel comfortable?"

The old man, who had seen much in his long life, looked up at Raghav and smiled. "Thank you for your offer, young man. But you are a fiend, and I know your ways. You do not seek to help me, but to mock me".

Raghav, surprised that the old man knew who he was, grinned maliciously. "Mock you? Oh, no, old man. I only wish to show you kindness. After all, you are an old, poor man, and what harm would it do to you?"

Without waiting for the old man’s response, Raghav knelt down and began to wash the old man’s feet, using water from a nearby stream. As he washed, he felt a strange sense of satisfaction. His cruelty was being disguised as kindness, and for a moment, he felt as though he was doing a noble thing.
